# Five proven principles for AI adoption

> 70% of AI pilots burn six months before being shelved. A 5-item go/no-go checklist kills them at the pitch, not at month 6.

## What

A 5-principle checklist for evaluating any proposed AI initiative before greenlighting it. The principles come from a large wealth-management firm's two years of internal AI rollouts (23 services in production, low-eight-figures/year measured effect on chat alone) and are now used as a go/no-go filter on new pitches.

## Why it matters

Each principle maps to a specific failure mode the firm paid for. Applying them costs nothing and kills 70% of AI initiatives that would otherwise burn six months before being quietly shelved. The "business owner required" principle alone saves entire pilots.

## End-to-end

1. **Add the five principles to your AI proposal template** as required questions.
2. **Block any pitch that can't name a business owner.**
3. **For "embedded vs standalone" debates, default to embedded.** The burden of proof is on the standalone advocate.
4. **Quarterly review of in-flight projects** against principles 1, 2, 3; pivot or kill the violators.

## Gotchas

- "We'll find an owner later" never happens. Make the business-owner sign-off a hard gate before any engineering hours are spent.
